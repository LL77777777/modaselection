New Earbuds? What to Check Before You Upgrade

Last updated: July 17, 2026 · By Moda Selection editors

Earbud launches are built around one dramatic improvement: smarter noise control, longer battery, clearer sound. The better question is quieter. Will this pair make your train ride, calls, walks, and desk hours noticeably easier?

Start with the shape of your day

Write down the three places you use earbuds most. Someone who wears them on a crowded commute needs easy controls and convincing noise reduction. Someone who takes calls from a quiet home office may care more about long-session comfort and a natural transparency mode. For a runner, a stable fit and sweat resistance move to the top of the list.

This simple exercise stops every premium feature from looking equally important. It also makes reviews easier to read: you can skip the sections that do not match your life.

Fit is not a footnote

Earbuds can sound excellent and still be a poor buy if they create pressure after forty minutes. Check how many tip sizes are included and whether third-party tips are easy to find. Read reviews from people who mention small ears, glasses, piercings, or long calls if those details apply to you.

A return policy matters here. Fit is personal and difficult to judge from dimensions. If a retailer does not accept opened earbuds for hygiene reasons, know that before ordering.

Treat battery numbers as a scenario

Battery life is often measured under controlled conditions. Noise control, calls, high volume, and certain spatial features can shorten it. Rather than chasing the largest number, make sure the earbuds cover your longest normal session with room to spare and that the case fits your charging habits.

Also check how the case charges. A pair that uses the same cable as your phone may be easier to live with than one that adds another cable to your bag. Wireless charging is convenient on a desk, but it is not essential if you rarely use a charging pad.

Listen to microphone tests, not adjectives

Good music performance does not guarantee clear calls. Look for recordings made in traffic, wind, a café, and a quiet room. You are listening for two things: whether speech remains understandable and whether noise processing makes the voice sound thin or robotic.

If calls are part of your working day, a less glamorous pair with reliable microphones can be more useful than a premium model tuned mainly for music.

Check the controls with cold hands and full attention

Controls should work when you are walking, carrying a bag, or wearing gloves—not only when you are sitting still. Physical buttons can be reassuring; touch controls can be quicker when they are well designed. Look for the ability to customize gestures and turn off the ones you trigger by accident.

Compatibility can be worth more than a sound upgrade

If you move between a phone and laptop, multipoint connection may save more frustration than a small change in sound quality. Check codec and feature support across the devices you actually own; some headline features work only inside one brand's ecosystem.

Find out whether replacement tips and single earbuds are available, how battery service works, and whether the companion app is required for basic settings. These details rarely lead the product page, but they shape the second year of ownership.

Know when the old pair is still enough

If your current earbuds remain comfortable, hold enough charge, and connect reliably, a new launch may not solve a real problem. Clean the charging contacts, replace worn tips, and reset the connection before assuming the hardware is finished.

A worthwhile upgrade should remove a frustration you encounter every week. “New” is not a use case.
